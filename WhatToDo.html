How to Identify A Phishing Email?

Check The Email Address

  1. Phishers often hide their real email addresses to look like they’re from reputable sources. Pay attention to slight variations in the sender’s email address.
  2. Legitimate emails: From well-known domains like @bank.com or @amazon.com.
  3. Phishing attempt: Look for unusual domain names like @service-support.com or @amazonsupport.net. The domain name might be similar to a real domain but with an extra character or misspelled.

Generic Greetings:

  1. Phishing emails often use a generic greeting such as “Dear Customer” or “Dear User” because the attacker does not know your actual Name or Gender.
  2. Legitimate emails: Most legitimate companies address you by your name (e.g., “Dear justify-content).
  3. However, Phishing attempts usually have generic greetings. These are red flags when identifying if an email is legitimate or not.

Suspicious Links

  1. Phishing emails usually have suspicious links that are designed to install malware on the computer or steal information when opened.
  2. Legitimate websites will have correct links when hovered over to check destination URL(e.g-//www.amazon.com)
  3. However, Phishing websites will show a different link that isn't the one that it says it goes to (e.g-//www.malwaredownload.com)
